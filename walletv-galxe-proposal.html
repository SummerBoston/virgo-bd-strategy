Why each task was chosen
Follow @WalletV — baseline audience building. Every follower is a future organic reach point for all subsequent campaigns, not just this one.
Like & repost — retweets push the campaign beyond the existing follower base into followers-of-followers. At 300 participants, a 5% follow-through rate means ~600 second-degree impressions for free.
Comment + tag 2 friends — the highest-leverage task in the entire quest. Each completed comment puts the campaign in front of 2 warm, soccer-interested audiences who weren't in the funnel. This is a built-in referral loop. 300 commenters × 2 tags = 600 organic impressions among qualified audiences at $0 media cost.
Join Telegram — converts social engagement into owned community. Telegram members receive future campaign announcements directly, building a retention channel that survives algorithm changes on X.
On-chain prediction — the only task that verifies real product usage. Galxe can validate this via wallet-level on-chain data, making it tamper-proof and giving us clean attribution for actual new traders.
Why we need Galxe + social tasks to drive traction

Wallet V's core campaign rewards (trading volume, PnL) only reach users who are already in crypto and already motivated to trade. The $666 social engagement layer on Galxe is what reaches the next ring — sports fans, soccer communities, and the friends-of-friends who've never used DeFi. Social platforms are the acquisition surface. The prediction tournament is the activation event. Without the social layer, we're competing for the same pool of existing DeFi users every other protocol is already targeting.

Why this campaign demonstrates BD competency
Multi-segment acquisition design. The four reward methods target four different user psychologies simultaneously — competitive high-volume traders, risk-tolerant profit-seekers, risk-averse first-timers who need a safety net, and social-first community members. This isn't one campaign for one audience; it's four funnels running in parallel during a single global event window.
Proven Galxe format, adapted with intent. The multi-task quest structure mirrors what's already working in this exact market during the same World Cup window — not an untested idea. Good BD identifies and adapts proven patterns rather than guessing from first principles, then adds the incremental twist (the @-tag mechanic, the referral layer) that gives it an edge.
The loss subsidy is the BD insight, not just a nice feature. The single biggest barrier to first-time DeFi prediction market users is "I'll lose my money." The loss subsidy directly neutralizes this objection at the product level. BD's job is to know what stops users at the door — and propose product/campaign structures that remove those barriers.
Social tasks build owned assets, not just traffic. Every follow, Telegram join, and retweet generated by this campaign is retained infrastructure — it survives the campaign end date and amplifies every future Wallet V campaign. One-time traffic campaigns leave nothing behind; social task campaigns compound.
World Cup timing is not accidental. This is a 39-day global event with billions of engaged viewers. Polymarket's prediction market already covers every match. The BD insight is that this creates a rare window where a "sports audience" and a "DeFi product" overlap naturally — without Wallet V having to manufacture the occasion or pay to create the interest.
